Growing Flowers & Memories

Welcome to my Family's Garden! Here holds a lot of memories of my family and a strong bond between my grandma and I as she has shown me how to take care of plants as I was growing up. Now it's my turn to teach you a few tips on how to care for plants and so you can grow not only your own garden, but your own memories and bonds with your loved ones!

Scroll down to read about these plants and how to take care of them.

Pink Miniature Garden Rose

These miniature pink garden roses are personally my favorite flowers in our garden. I love how they are tiny, but still bring a vibrant pop of color!

Tips to Grow Them

At least 6 hours of morning sunlight
Water the roots area directly
Use soil that is rich in nutrients

Yellow Rose

Yellow roses are my mom's absolute favorite flower! On any special occasions such as her birthday or an anniversary, my dad will always bring her a dozen yellow roses.

Tips to Grow Them

Keep in well-drained soil
Direct sunlight

Purple Freesia

Purple freesias remind me a lot of my sister because freesia flowers are her favorite flowers and purple is her favorite color. It truly represents her in our garden!

Tips to Grow Them

Consistently damp soil (not soggy or root may rot)
Grow bestt during spring season
Utilize fertilizer when the bud starts to form

Red Geranium

A fun fact about red geraniums are a great plant to have as they are symbolic of protection, good health, and happiness. Definitely a key plant to have outside in your garden to protect you and your loved ones!

Tips to Grow Them

Keep in well lit areas with some shade
Around 5-6 hours of sun (preferably in the morning)
Take off wilted flowers to promote more blooming
